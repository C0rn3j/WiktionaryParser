==English==

===Etymology===
From {{der|en|fro|amirail}}, {{m|fro|amiral}} (modern French {{m|fr|amiral}}), from {{der|en|ar|أَمِيرُ البَحْر||commander of the fleet}}. Later associated with {{m|en|admirable}}. Akin to {{m|en|amir}}, {{m|en|Amir}} and {{m|en|emir}}.

First recorded in English September, 1300, to refer to Gerard Allard of {{w|Winchelsea}}, referred to as “Admiral of the Fleet of the {{w|Cinque Ports}}”.<ref>The Mastery of the Sea, by Cyril Field, [http://books.google.com/books?id=rCWEeUgtXp0C&pg=PA234&dq=%22Gervase+Allard%22+admiral page 234]</ref><ref>{{R:Online Etymology Dictionary}}</ref>

===Pronunciation===
* {{a|UK}} {{IPA|/ˈæd.mə.ɹəl/|lang=en}}
* {{a|US}} {{IPA|/ˈæd.mə.ɹəl/|/ˈæd.mɚl̩/|lang=en}}
* {{audio|en-us-admiral.ogg|Audio (US)|lang=en}}

===Noun===
{{en-noun}}

# A naval officer of the highest rank; the commander of a country's naval forces.
# A naval officer of high rank, immediately below [[Admiral of the Fleet]]; the commander of a fleet or squadron.
# A [[flag officer]] in the [[United States Navy]] or [[United States Coast Guard|Coast Guard]] of a grade superior to [[vice admiral]] and junior to [[admiral of the fleet]] (when that grade is used). An admiral is equal in grade or rank to a four star [[general]].
# The ship which carries the admiral, the [[flagship]]; also, the most considerable ship of a fleet.
# {{lb|en|obsolete}} A prince or Saracen leader under the Sultan.
# Any of various nymphalid [[butterflies]] of Europe and America, especially a [[red admiral]] or [[white admiral]].
