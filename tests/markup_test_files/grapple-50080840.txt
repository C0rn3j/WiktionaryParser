===Etymology 1===
From {{derived|en|enm|*grapplen||to seize, lay hold of}}, from {{derived|en|ang|*græpplian||to seize}} (compare {{cog|ang|ġegræppian||to seize}}), from {{derived|en|gem-pro|*graipilōną}}, {{m|gem-pro|*grabbalōną||to seize}}, from {{derived|en|ine-pro|*ghreb(h)-}}, {{m|ine-pro|*ghrab(h)-||to take, seize, rake}}, equivalent to {{suffix|en|grab|le}}. Cognate with {{cog|nl|grabbelen||to grope, scramble, scrabble}}, {{cog|de|grabbeln||to rummage, grope about}} and {{m|de|grapsen}}, {{m|de|grapschen||to seize, grasp, grabble}}. Influenced in some senses by {{m|en|grapple||tool with claws or hooks|pos=noun}} ([[#Noun|see below]]). See further at ''{{l|en|grasp}}''.

====Verb====
{{en-verb|grappl}}

# {{lb|en|transitive}} To [[seize]] something and [[hold]] it [[firmly]].
# {{lb|en|transitive|figuratively}} Normally used with ''[[with]]'': to [[ponder]] and [[intensely]] [[evaluate]] a problem.
#: {{ux|en|to '''grapple''' with one's conscience}}
# {{lb|en|transitive}} To [[fasten]], as with a [[#Noun|grapple]]; to [[fix]]; to [[join]] [[indissolubly]].
#* Hakluyt
#*: The gallies were '''grappled''' to the Centurion.
#* Shakespeare
#*: '''Grapple''' them to thy soul with hoops of steel.
# {{lb|en|intransitive}} To use a grapple.
# {{lb|en|intransitive}} To [[wrestle]] or [[tussle]].

===Etymology 2===
[[File:Grappling hook 2 (PSF).png|thumb|A [[grappling hook]], which is a type of grapple]]
[[File:FMIB 40673 Grapple.jpeg|thumb|A grapple, or [[grapnel]] anchor]]

From {{derived|en|enm|*grapple}}, {{m|enm|*graple}}, from {{derived|en|fro|grappil||a ship's grapple}} (compare {{cog|fro|grappin||hook}}), from {{derived|en|fro|grape}}, {{m|fro|grappe}}, {{m|fro|crape||hook}}, of {{cog|gem}} origin, from Old {{derived|en|frk|*krappō||hook}}, from {{derived|en|gem-pro|*krappô}}, {{m|gem-pro|*krappą||hook}}, from {{derived|en|ine-pro|*grep-||hook}}, {{m|ine-pro|*gremb-||crooked, uneven}}, from {{derived|en|ine-pro|*ger-||to turn, bend, twist}}. See further at {{m|en|grape}}.
