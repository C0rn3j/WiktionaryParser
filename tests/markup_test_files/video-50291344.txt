==English==

===Etymology===
{{PIE root|en|weyd}}
Borrowed from {{bor|en|la|videō||I see}}.

===Pronunciation===
* {{IPA|/ˈvɪdiˌoʊ/|lang=en}}
* {{audio|En-us-video.ogg|Audio (US)|lang=en}}

===Noun===
{{en-noun|~|s}}
[[File:VHS cassette tape 05.JPG|thumb|right|A [[VHS]] '''video''' casette]]

# [[television|Television]], [[television show]], [[movie]].
# A short film clip, with or without [[audio]] (as in a [[music video]], or one of the plethora of user-generated short movies on sites such as [[YouTube]]).
# Motion picture stored on [[VHS]] or some other format.
# {{lb|en|dated}} [[VHS]].

====Usage notes====
* {{m|en|video|Video}} is used in contrast with {{m|en|audio}}, which is sound only.
* The plural {{m|en||videmus}} is rare and used for humorous effect. It is the first-person plural form of the Latin verb ("we see") in the same way that {{m|la||video}} is the singular.
